Resilient webhook ingestion

Accept third-party webhooks without losing events when downstream is slow or flaky.

Overview

We terminate inbound webhooks at a thin gateway that does nothing but authenticate the caller and durably enqueue the raw payload. A pool of workers drains the queue and applies business logic. The gateway stays fast and boring; all the retry complexity lives in the worker.

Retry strategy

Transient failures are the common case here, not the exception. Delivery to the downstream store can fail transiently, so the worker does not fail the whole event on the first error. Instead it will retry with exponential backoff, starting at 200ms and doubling until a cap.

When a payload cannot be delivered after repeated attempts it moves to a dead-letter queue for later inspection, rather than blocking the head of the line.

Failure handling

The worker keeps draining so one poison message never stalls the pipeline; a dead letter is inspected out of band and replayed once the downstream recovers.
